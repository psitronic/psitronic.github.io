# AI Consulting Website Requirements

**Date:** 2026-06-01  
**Status:** Ready for planning

## Problem

Andrey Sidorenko is launching an AI consulting practice (Andrey Sidorenko, e.U.) targeting SMEs and tech companies in DACH and Eastern Europe. He needs a professional website that establishes credibility and generates inbound leads — without looking like the generic, buzzword-heavy AI consultant sites that saturate the market.

## Outcome

A single-page consulting website that positions Andrey as a **deep technical specialist who ships production AI fast**. Visitors should understand within seconds: what he does, why he's credible, and how to contact him.

## Target Audience

- SMEs and tech startups in DACH region and Eastern Europe
- Technical decision-makers (CTOs, engineering leads) evaluating AI development partners
- Companies needing rapid prototyping, not months-long discovery phases

## Core Sections

### 1. Hero
- Bold headline communicating the core value prop
- 1-2 sentence supporting text — specific, not abstract
- Example direction: "I build production AI systems" — not "Transforming businesses with AI"

### 2. About
- Brief bio (3-4 sentences)
- Professional headshot
- Key credentials: PhD in Physics (Moscow State University), Head of AI Research at MOSTLY AI (2021-2026), 15+ years AI/ML experience
- Tone: confident but not boastful

### 3. Services
- 4-item grid with equal visual weight:
  1. **AI Prototype Sprint** — 2-4 week intensive for functional proof of concept
  2. **AI Product Development** — Production-ready AI applications and systems
  3. **AI System Integration** — Embedding AI into existing infrastructure
  4. **Technical AI Training** — Workshops for development teams
- Each service: icon + title + 1-2 sentence description
- No pricing on public site

### 4. Portfolio / Proof
- **Published Research:** Links to arXiv papers (TabularARGN, LLM tabular generation, benchmarking framework)
- **Open Source:** GitHub profile/repos
- **MOSTLY AI Work:** Any public blog posts, conference talks, or press
- Framed as evidence of technical depth, not a traditional "client logos" section

### 5. Contact
- Email address (primary)
- LinkedIn profile link
- Social links in footer (GitHub, Google Scholar, Twitter if applicable)
- Location: Vienna, Austria

## Visual Direction

**Style:** Engineering pragmatism
- Functional, direct, no-nonsense
- Reference: well-designed developer tools, technical documentation
- Monospace font accents
- Muted color palette (avoid bright gradients, neon accents)
- High contrast for readability
- Generous whitespace

**Explicitly avoid:**
- Gradient blobs and abstract "neural network" imagery
- Stock photography
- Generic AI iconography (brains, robots, circuit patterns)
- Busy layouts with competing visual elements

## Messaging Principles

1. **Lead with specifics:** Name actual projects (TabularARGN), actual roles (Head of AI Research), actual outcomes
2. **No hollow buzzwords:** Avoid "cutting-edge," "transformative," "unlock potential," "leverage AI"
3. **Direct voice:** First person ("I build...") not corporate third person ("We deliver...")
4. **Credibility through evidence:** Link to papers, code, public talks — let the work speak

## Technical Constraints

- Static site (will be hosted on GitHub Pages alongside existing portfolio)
- No backend required (email link, not contact form)
- Must work on mobile
- Can coexist with or replace existing `index.html` — decision for planning phase

## Out of Scope

- Multi-page site architecture
- Blog or content management
- Contact forms or calendar booking integrations
- German language version (can be added later)
- Client testimonials (no clients yet — launching Jan 2027)
- Pricing page

## Success Criteria

1. A visitor can understand what Andrey does within 5 seconds of landing
2. Technical decision-makers find credible evidence (papers, GitHub, track record)
3. The site looks distinctly professional — not template-generic
4. Clear path to contact (email, LinkedIn)
